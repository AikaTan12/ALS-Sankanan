ALS provides a second chance for individuals who were unable to complete their formal education.

What is Alternative Learning System (ALS)?:

The Alternative Learning System (ALS) is an educational program aimed at individuals who did not complete formal schooling. It offers a flexible and personalized learning approach, accommodating various learning styles and paces. ALS provides diverse subjects and levels, enabling students to pursue a high school equivalency diploma or certifications. The curriculum focuses on practical skills relevant to learners' lives and future goals, with learning environments that may include in-person or online instruction. The primary goal of ALS is to ensure equitable access to education and empower individuals to enhance their lives through learning.

Vision:

to provide equitable access to quality education for all, regardless of age, location, or circumstances, empowering individuals to improve their lives and contribute to society. This means ensuring that everyone has the opportunity to complete their basic education and acquire the skills needed to succeed in life.

Mission:

"To provide access to quality basic education for out-of-school children, youth, and adults who are unable to attend formal school.".
